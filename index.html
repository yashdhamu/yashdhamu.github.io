About Me.

I am Yash Ashok Dhamecha, a dedicated professional driven by a profound interest in computer science, particularly in the domains of Software development. Pursing a Master's in Computer Science from Arizona State University and a Bachelor's in Information Technology from College Of Engineering, Pune, I have honed a versatile skill set encompassing various programming languages, frameworks, and developer tools.

During my tenure as a Software Developer at Barclays Pune, I spearheaded several transformative projects. Notably, I contributed to the seamless migration of mainframe applications, driving substantial cost savings while enhancing data warehouse operations. Additionally, I implemented robust CI/CD pipelines, enhancing our development processes for greater efficiency. Augmenting this, I introduced automated testing solutions, reducing manual efforts and fortifying software reliability. These initiatives collectively underscore my proficiency in optimizing operations and delivering tangible, bottom-line impact.

Academically, my projects in video summarization and gesture recognition highlight my expertise in machine learning and computer vision. This is exemplified in my work on projects like sign language recognition, where I bridged communication gaps for individuals with disabilities, and in the development of an optimized algorithm for Sudoku solving, dramatically reducing solving times for standard 9x9 grids. Additionally, I have also undertaken backend projects involving distributed banking systems. This involved designing and implementing robust architectures that ensure secure and efficient transaction handling across multiple nodes.

Beyond technical prowess, I have demonstrated strong leadership and teamwork skills in various robotics competitions and street play events. These experiences have equipped me with the ability to manage budgets, inspire teams, and drive innovative solutions, resulting in top placements in several competitions.

Currently, I serve as a Grader for the Theory of Computation course at Arizona State University. This role allows me to channel my dedication to academic excellence and support fellow students in their educational journey.

In essence, I bring forth a combination of a solid academic foundation, technical proficiency, and a fervor for innovation. My aspiration to make a positive impact in the realms of computer science and personal finance aligns seamlessly with organizations that share a vision for growth, innovation, and societal well-being. I look forward to leveraging my skills and passion to contribute meaningfully to such endeavors.

GestureMaster

Enabling Human-Computer Interaction

The Gesture Recognition project showcases my expertise in advanced technologies such as machine learning and computer vision. By implementing the VGG16 model and Python, I developed a prototype for accurate gesture recognition, enabling a deeper level of human-computer interaction. Project Link.

Semantic Video Summarizer

For Enhanced Understanding

This advanced video processing project employs cutting-edge techniques, including feature vector clustering and semantic factorization. It enhances frame analysis, grouping them based on both inherent features and semantic content. The resulting concept clusters serve as representative frames, offering a refined approach to video summarization with potential applications in content analysis and information retrieval.

Distributed Banking System

Transactions Across Distributed Branches

This project implements a distributed banking system using gRPC, with customers making deposit and withdrawal requests to branches. Each customer and branch runs as a separate process, communicating through gRPC stubs. Events and interactions are logged for both customers and branches, providing a comprehensive view of the system's operations.
